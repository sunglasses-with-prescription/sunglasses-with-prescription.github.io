Understanding Presbyopia: The Ubiquitous Age-Related Vision Impairment

Eyeglasses for near vision are crucial for countless individuals worldwide. Presbyopia, often referred to as age-related farsightedness, is a natural part of aging where the eye's lens becomes less flexible. This reduction in flexibility impairs the eye's ability to change focus from distant to near objects, affecting a significant portion of the adult population.

The Widespread Impact on Daily Life and Economic Participation

Primary Cause of Impairment: Identified as the leading cause of vision impairment globally, typically affecting individuals from age 50.

Functional Limitations: Can severely restrict engagement in tasks requiring near vision, such as reading, sewing, or intricate handcrafts.

The prevalence of presbyopia makes it a leading cause of vision impairment globally, typically affecting individuals from around age 50.
